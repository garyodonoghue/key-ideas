Homelessness in Ireland



Homelessness can mean sleeping rough, staying in emergency hostels or shelters, staying in temporary bed and breakfast accommodation or staying with friends and relatives when there is nowhere else to go. For people experiencing homelessness, daily life involves a lack of security, a lack of belonging and exposure to cold, sickness and isolation. The current economic climate means more people are at risk of homelessness than ever before. With further cutbacks in health, education, welfare services and training more people will become homeless. Focus Ireland estimates that there are up to 5,000 people at any one time who are homeless in Ireland. This figure has been exceeded for the first time this year and approximately 1,600 of those who are homeless are children. At the moment there is no explicit right to housing in the Constitution or in Irish Law.
