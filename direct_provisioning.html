Direct Provisioning



Direct provision is a system which is intended to provide for the welfare of asylum seekers and their families as they await decisions on their asylum application. It was designed as a means of meeting the basic needs of food and shelter for asylum seekers directly while their claims for refugee status are being processed. Direct provision commenced on 10 April, 2000. Since that time, asylum seekers in the system have received three meals a day, accommodation and personal allowances. These personal allowances are currently €19.10 per adult and €15.60 per child per week. Until the 6th of January 2016, the child allowance was €9.60. Groups including Barnardos, Children’s Rights Alliance, and the ISPCC have stated that the increase will have little benefit, and falls significantly short of recommendations. Approximately 4500 people live in direct provision, across 35 centres throughout the country. The centres, which include former hostels, hotels and a mobile home park, are run by private contractors who receive about €50 million in State funding annually. The direct provision system has been heavily criticised by human rights campaigners. The main concerns raised include: The duration for which asylum seekers remain in the system. The majority of asylum seekers spend 3 years in Direct Provision, with a significant number waiting for 7 years or more. The restrictive conditions under which residents live. For example, asylum seekers must eat in a canteen at certain times and are not allowed to cook in their own room. Lack of personal space and privacy. Single residents share a room with several other adults, and parents live in one room with their children. Prohibition from working. Ireland is only one of two EU countries in which asylum seekers are prohibited from working after a designated period. Lack of access to third level education. Asylum seekers are not entitled to free education, meaning that those who wish to go to third-level would have to pay fees in excess of €10,000. A fee which is unattainable when the weekly personal allowance of adults and children is €19.10 and €15.60 respectively.
