Meet the KEY Ideas and Decisions team!


Laura Finnegan



Laura is a 2nd year medical student with the Graduate Entry Medical School at the University of Limerick. She has been involved with various youth organisations since the age of 15. Most notably Laura served on the National Committee for the European Youth Parliament for two years and now serves as an Alumni Advisor for the organisation. Laura is also serving on the University of Limerick’s Medical Society Committee as Charity Officer for the 2015/2016 term. Laura is particularly passionate about women’s rights, and all issues relating to equality.



Chris Noone



Chris Noone is a PhD Candidate in the School of Psychology at NUI Galway. His work focuses on the relationship between mindfulness and critical thinking. He has been active in student representation at local, national and international levels including serving on the Board of Management of the European Federation of Psychology Students’ Associations. This and work with colleagues at NUIG has allowed him to explore advocacy issues, citizen engagement and the role psychology can play in facilitating advocacy, engagement and policy design. Chris is passionate about citizen engagement and is hoping to further his knowledge and experience of this area.



Gary O' Donoghue



Gary O’ Donoghue is a Software Engineer working in Galway. His main interests in school were related to Mathematics and technical subjects and so he went on to study Electronic and Computer Engineering in NUI Galway. After that, he undertook a graduate training course with Fidelity Investments for 6 months, and upon completion, went on to work full time for them, where he works today.



Owen Harney



Owen Harney is a PhD Candidate in the School of Psychology at NUI Galway. His research interests centre on collaborative learning, specifically, how groups can learn and work together effectively. Through this line of research and related community work in NUIG, Owen has become increasingly interested in citizen engagement. Owen has been active in student representation, serving on the Psychological Society of Ireland Student Affairs Group (PSISAG), and as Member Representative for PSISAG on the European Federation of Psychology Students’ Associations (EFPSA). Owen hopes to combine his interests in psychology and citizen engagement in this programme, and future projects.
