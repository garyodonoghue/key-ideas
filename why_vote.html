Why Vote?




It may sometimes seem like voting is not worthwhile but in fact, making an informed choice regarding your local candidates is vital to ensure you have done your best to put people in power who share your goals for society. It is especially important that we as young vote because it has been shown that those who vote tend be older, wealthier and supporters of traditional parties. It is no surprise then that their interests are looked after by successive governments. However, when young people turn up to vote en masse, it can change the country for the better as we saw with last year’s marriage referendum. If you are not happy with how politicians have run the country recently, the solution is not to ignore them by not voting at all. This just gives more power to those that put elected those politicians in the first place.The solution is to find credible candidates that offer an alternative. If young people show their determination to turn up to vote in the upcoming general election, politicians will have no choice but to listen to our concerns about Irish society. While the Union of Students of Ireland has done a fantastic job at registering students to vote, not all young people are students and we believe all young people deserve to have their voice heard in this election campaign. On the other hand, if young people turning out well below other groups, politicians will pay less attention to what young people want to see happen in society. In this way who votes has a powerful impact on public policy, government and ultimately the society we live in. How to Vote Check the register Go to checktheregister.ie. All you need to do is enter your address and name and you will see whether your details are on the register or not. If you are not on the register, click on the FAQs button in the top-right corner of the screen. Here you find links to the forms you need to print and fill out. Post the filled out form to your local authority office. you can find the addresses to all the local authority offices in Ireland here. Make an informed decision about your vote It is important to find out which of your local candidates is best suited to represent you. You can find some tips on how to do this here (link to our article). Receive your polling card When you receive your polling card, look at which polling station you will be using. Sometimes you will need to use online maps to figure out where they are. It's a good idea to make a plan on how you’ll get there on election day. Attend the polling station It's a good idea to make a plan in advance on how you’ll get there on election day. Polling stations open from 7am to 10pm. Stay in touch with your elected representative After the election, your local TD is responsible for representing you in the Dáil. You should feel comfortable contacting them with your concerns.
